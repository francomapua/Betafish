​I. TIME MANAGEMENT
​The Master Clock: Each player begins the game with exactly 5 minutes (300 seconds) of game time.

​Elimination: If a player’s timer reaches zero (0:00), they immediately lose the game regardless of life total or board state.

​Active Clock: The active player’s timer counts down continuously until they explicitly pass the turn.

​Intervention & Responses: A player may not influence the game (manipulate the board, activate abilities, or comment on the state) unless their timer is running. To take an action on another player's turn:
​The responding player must pause the active player's clock.
​The responding player must start their own clock before acting.
​II. PRIORITY & THE STACK
​The 2-Second Resolution: The stack is considered resolved if no player influences it for more than 2 seconds.
​Passing Priority: During any step where priority is explicitly passed (e.g., end of phases or the Declare Blockers step), any player who takes no action within 2 seconds is considered to have passed priority.
​Damage Resolution: All calculations and physical adjustments for damage (updating life totals, adding/removing counters) run on the damage-dealer's clock.
​III. INFORMATION & CLARITY
​Universal Knowledge: Players play all cards assuming their opponents have full knowledge of every card's text and Oracle rulings.
​The "5-Second Pause": A player may call a "Rule 6 Pause" for 5 seconds to have another player explain their card (stats, keywords, or specific effects). This is the only time the game clock may be fully stopped for discussion.
​Physical Integrity: Players must keep their hands clear of the board unless their clock is running to ensure clarity of the "Active Player."
​IV. SPEED ETIQUETTE
​Search Fast: If searching a library, find your card and shortcut the shuffle.
​No Stalling: Using the 5-second pause to "think" rather than clarify a card is considered unsportsmanlike.
​Decisive Plays: If you can't do the math in time, you can't make the play.
