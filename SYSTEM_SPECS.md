# Betafish System Specification

## 1. Overview & Core Mechanics
Betafish is a client-side React SPA designed for Magic: The Gathering "goldfishing." Users test strategies against simulated, automated opponents. The game operates on a turn-based loop where users can deal damage, pass turns, and arbitrate opponent actions. Opponents dropping to 0 health are eliminated; opponents successfully resolving all pieces of their designated wincon win the game.

## 2. State Management & Persistence
* **Global Store**: Utilizes Zustand or Redux Toolkit.
* **State Structure**: Tracks `gameMeta` (autogenerated name, settings), `entities` (health, active status, assembled wincons for player and opponents), and `turnManager` (turn order, active action queue).
* **Save/Load**: The entire highly-serializable state object is dumped into browser `localStorage` to facilitate the Welcome Screen's "Load Game" functionality.
* **Undo System**: Implements a Command Pattern or state-snapshot array that logs every resolution, health change, and phase shift, allowing the user to reverse actions back to turn one.

## 3. Opponent AI & Action Resolution
* **Weighted Logic**: During an opponent's turn, the system selects actions from their specific `OpponentProfile`. Selection probability is dynamically calculated using the action's `base_weight` modified by the current `turn_number` and the opponent's `current_health`.
* **The "Stack" & Arbitration**: When an opponent attempts an action or plays a wincon piece, the action enters a pending state. The UI pauses and prompts the user, who acts as the sole arbiter to either successfully negate the action or allow it to fully resolve.

## 4. Expected User Experience (UX) & Layout
* **Welcome & Setup**: A clean, centered landing page with distinct "New Game" and "Load Game" options. Starting a new game opens a step-by-step modal to select opponent profiles, adjust life totals, and randomize the turn order.
* **The Game Board**: The UI is divided into three main zones. The top or sides house the **Opponent Dashboards**, displaying their current health and visible wincon progress. The bottom area is the **Player Console**, featuring your life total and prominent action buttons like "Pass Turn" or "Deal Damage". 
* **The Stack & Action Log**: A central or togglable side-panel chronologically tracks all moves. When an opponent initiates an action, the game pauses; the pending action is highlighted prominently with large, clear "Resolve" or "Negate" arbitration buttons.
* **Fluid Navigation**: The "Undo" function is accessible via a persistent, easily reachable button on the main board, allowing you to instantly reverse the game state without navigating through secondary menus.
